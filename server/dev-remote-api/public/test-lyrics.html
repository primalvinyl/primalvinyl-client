About “Shape of My Heart”

“Shape of My Heart” is the 9th track (10th on international copies) on Ten Summoner’s Tales. It was co-written by Dominic Miller, Sting’s guitarist, which makes it one of the few songs not written by Sting himself in his solo career.

Sting would later revisit the song with pop singer Craig David.

It is famously featured in the end credits of 1994’s Léon: The Professional.

  • How have other artists interpreted the song more recently?

    After filing a lawsuit, Sting, was awarded 85% of the revenue from Juice WRLD’s “Lucid Dreams”, which interpolated the 67 year old rock star’s 1993 hit Shape of my Heart

    Despite his initial frustration, Juice WRLD seems to have made peace with the situation.

    Following a series of angry since-deleted tweets about the seizure from song producer Nick Mira, WRLD tweeted,

    Lost millions made millions…the song impacted to many ppl in a good way for me to be upset over it..there’s always more money to be made and I will make it so ❤️❤️

    He later told NME,

    That song is so much more expensive than money and what money can buy,… It’s so much more touching than what money could touch. That song really saved lives. And people are sitting here telling me, ‘Blah blah blah, 90% this, 90% that.’ They bogus fucks. No, bro. Without Sting, this wouldn’t even be a song. I don’t really trip on money, especially with a song like that, with the reaction it’s had from everybody.

    WRLD may have also referenced the situation in “Armed and Dangerous,” which includes the lines,

    Pay up that cash, you owe me, yeah, huh
    Bitch, I need it
    Matter fact, fuck that shit, I’m rich (Uh)
    You can keep it

    For his part, Sting described Juice WRLD’s song as “a beautiful interpretation” of his song, adding that the seized royalties with “put my grandkids through college.”

  • From what movie?

    Sting’s “Shape Of My Heart” was featured in the end credits of the English-language French film Leon: The Professional by Luc Besson starring Jean Reno and a very young Natalie Portman.

  • What have the artists said about the song?

    In an interview with Sting he said the following:

    The derivation of playing cards are actually tarot cards, and they are thousands of years old, And the suits of the cards – diamonds mean money, clubs are weapons, spades are swords and the hearts are love – and I got more involved in this thing. The shape of the heart is not the shape of the human heart so there’s a kind of conflict there between reality and fantasy.
